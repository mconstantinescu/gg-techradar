About the Technology Portal

The Gunvor Group Technology Portal unifies three complementary instruments that together form our technology governance framework. It helps engineering teams discover technologies, understand decision rationale, and follow golden-path documentation.

Technology Radar

A landscape view of technologies the organisation tracks, evaluates, and recommends across four quadrants and four adoption rings.

Decision Records

Enterprise-wide TDRs and project-level ADRs — organised across six domains and four lifecycle stages.

Technical Documentation

Architecture guidelines, golden paths, standard operating procedures, and technology policies.

Governance Framework

Together, these three pillars form the governance chain: Radar (what we track) → Decisions (why we chose) → Docs (how we implement).

Instrument Purpose Audience
Technology Radar Landscape view of technologies we track — the "what" All engineering staff
Technology Decision Record (TDR) Global decision set by the TAG — enterprise-wide mandates and guardrails TAG, Architecture Board, all teams
Architecture Decision Record (ADR) Project- or programme-level decision linked to ASRs Project / product teams
Technical Documentation Blueprints, golden paths, SOPs, policies & whitepapers — the "how" All engineering staff

The 4-Stage Lifecycle (Rings)

Ring Stage Meaning
Assess Research Under research or proof-of-concept. No production impact allowed yet.
Trial Validate Low-risk production use. Limited to specific projects or early-adopter teams.
Standard Golden Path Recommended for all new workloads. The default choice unless a documented exemption exists.
Sunset Retire Formally deprecated. No new implementations. Migration or exit plan required.

  1. Radar → ADR: A team evaluates a technology visible on the radar and writes an ADR to adopt it for their project.
  2. ADR → TDR: When multiple projects independently adopt the same technology, the TAG formalises it as an enterprise-wide TDR.
  3. TDR → Radar: The new TDR updates the radar, signalling the technology's endorsed status organisation-wide.
  4. Decisions → Docs: Adopted technologies and patterns are documented as golden paths, SOPs, and blueprints in the Documentation section.

Who Decides?

TDR — Technology Advisory Group (TAG)

  • Scope: Enterprise / Global
  • Binding: All new services; existing services must comply on major version change or within 12 months
  • Examples: Primary cloud provider, enterprise identity provider, CI/CD platform

ADR — Project Stakeholders & SMEs

  • Scope: Project or Product/Programme
  • Anchored in: Architecturally Significant Requirements (ASRs)
  • Examples: Event-driven order processing, service-level database choice

Compliance & Exemptions

  • New services must comply with all Standard-ring TDRs at inception.
  • Existing services must comply on major version change or within 12 months of ratification.
  • Teams may request a temporary exemption via the TAG with documented justification and a compliance roadmap.
  • Project ADRs must not contradict Standard-ring enterprise TDRs — the TDR takes precedence.

Contributing

Content is managed across three Azure DevOps repositories within the Technology project:

  • Radar — technology radar blips (markdown in radar/)
  • Decisions — TDRs and ADRs (markdown in tdr/)
  • Documents — technical documentation (markdown in docs/)

Open a pull request in the relevant repository. TDRs require TAG review; ADRs require project stakeholder review; documents follow standard PR approval process.
